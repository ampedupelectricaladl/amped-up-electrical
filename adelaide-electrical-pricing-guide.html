What's Typically Included in Electrical Quotes?

A proper fixed-price quote from a licensed Adelaide electrician should include:

  • Labor costs: Electrician's time, including travel to/from your property
  • Materials & fittings: Cable, switches, power points, circuit breakers, etc.
  • Testing & certification: RCD testing, insulation testing, COES lodged with SafeWork SA
  • Clean-up: Removal of old fittings, vacuuming, patching (basic wall touch-ups)
  • Warranty: Workmanship warranty (typically 12 months, varies by electrician)

What's usually NOT included:

  • Painting/plastering (beyond basic patching)
  • Structural modifications (cutting joists, major wall alterations)
  • Permit fees (if council permits required for commercial work)
  • Trenching/excavation for underground cable runs
  • Appliances/fittings you supply (unless installation included in quote)

Fixed Price vs Hourly: Which Is Better?

Fixed price quotes are standard for defined jobs:

  • ‚úÖ You know the total cost upfront
  • ‚úÖ No surprises if the job takes longer than expected
  • ‚úÖ Electrician is motivated to work efficiently
  • ‚ùå May cost more if job is simpler than anticipated

Hourly rates suit fault-finding and complex diagnostics:

  • ‚úÖ Only pay for actual time spent
  • ‚úÖ Fairer if problem is quick to fix
  • ‚ùå Final cost unknown until job done
  • ‚ùå Risk of slow work or "time padding"

Recommendation: Get fixed price quotes whenever possible. Use hourly only for fault-finding where the problem isn't clear upfront (e.g., intermittent tripping, mystery power loss).

Why Do Electrician Quotes Vary So Much?

You might get three quotes for the same job ranging from $800 to $2,200. Here's why:

1. Licensing & Insurance

Licensed electricians carry public liability insurance ($10M-$20M coverage), workers comp insurance, and pay for annual licensing fees. Unlicensed "handymen" doing electrical work have none of this‚Äîtheir quotes are cheaper because they're cutting corners and breaking the law.

2. Materials Quality

Budget quote: Generic Chinese power points ($5-$8), thin cable, basic switches
Premium quote: Clipsal/HPM fittings ($15-$25), proper cable sizing, quality circuit breakers

The difference? Budget materials fail faster, look worse, and may not meet AS/NZS 3000 standards.

3. Experience & Skill

A 20-year licensed electrician charges more than a fresh apprentice‚Äîbut the experienced sparky finishes faster, solves problems on the spot, and delivers cleaner work. You're paying for competence, not just time.

4. Business Overheads

Professional electricians invest in:

  • Quality tools & test equipment ($10K+ investment)
  • Fully stocked work vans (no delays waiting for parts)
  • Professional communication (prompt quotes, on-time arrival)
  • Ongoing training & certifications
  • Customer service systems (booking, follow-ups, warranties)

Budget operators skip these costs‚Äîwhich is why they're cheaper, but also why they're unreliable.

5. Reputation & Demand

Electricians with 50+ five-star Google reviews can charge premium rates because customers trust them. If you're booked out 2 weeks in advance, you don't need to discount to compete with dodgy $80/hr sparkies.

Red Flags: When a Quote Is TOO Cheap

If a quote is 30-50% below market rates, ask why. Common red flags:

  • ‚ùå No ABN or license number provided
  • ‚ùå Cash-only payment (avoiding tax)
  • ‚ùå No written quote or contract
  • ‚ùå Won't provide Certificate of Electrical Safety
  • ‚ùå Can't show proof of insurance
  • ‚ùå Pressures you to decide immediately

Saving $300 on a switchboard upgrade isn't worth it if your house burns down and insurance won't cover it because the work was illegal.

How to Get Accurate Quotes

For small jobs (power points, switches, ceiling fans):

  • Send photos of the area and existing setup
  • Describe exactly what you want (e.g., "install double GPO in laundry, brick wall, 5m from switchboard")
  • Ask if price includes materials or if you're supplying them
  • Confirm callout fees (some charge $50-$100 minimum even for quotes)

For large jobs (switchboard upgrades, full house rewiring):

  • Request on-site assessment (can't accurately quote without inspecting)
  • Get 2-3 written quotes to compare
  • Ask about materials brands (Clipsal vs generic matters)
  • Confirm timelines (SA Power Networks coordination for switchboards)
  • Check what's included (testing, certification, clean-up)

Ways to Save Money on Electrical Work (Without Compromising Safety)

  • Bundle jobs: Get multiple power points installed in one visit rather than separate callouts
  • Supply your own fittings: If you want designer switches/lights, buy them yourself (electrician charges less for install-only)
  • Schedule during business hours: Avoid after-hours emergency rates unless genuinely urgent
  • Combine with other trades: Coordinate electrician with plumber/builder to minimize site visits
  • Get quotes before committing: Don't accept vague "I'll need to see it" without at least a rough estimate
  • Maintain proactively: Annual inspections prevent expensive emergency repairs

Don't try to save by:

  • ‚ùå Hiring unlicensed electricians (illegal, voids insurance)
  • ‚ùå DIY electrical work (illegal in SA, extremely dangerous)
  • ‚ùå Accepting quotes without COES certification
  • ‚ùå Using cheap, non-compliant materials

Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Adelaide Electrician

  1. "What's your SA electrical license number?" (It's public record‚Äîlegitimate sparkies provide it immediately)
  2. "Do you have public liability insurance?" (Should be $10M+ coverage minimum)
  3. "Will you provide a Certificate of Electrical Safety?" (Legally required‚Äîif they say no, walk away)
  4. "How long have you been a licensed electrician?" (Experience matters for complex jobs)
  5. "What brands of materials do you use?" (Clipsal/HPM = quality; no-name brands = cheaper but risky)
  6. "Do you subcontract work?" (Owner-operated = accountable; subcontractors = variable quality)
  7. "What's your warranty on workmanship?" (12 months is standard)
  8. "Can I see recent examples of similar jobs?" (Photos, references, Google reviews)
