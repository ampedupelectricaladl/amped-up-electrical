Quick Decision Guide: Plumber or Electrician?

Call a Plumber if:

  • Water is leaking or not flowing
  • Drains are blocked
  • Toilets won't flush
  • Gas appliances need connecting
  • Hot water system plumbing issues
  • Taps need replacing

Call an Electrician if:

  • Power is out or circuits are tripping
  • Lights aren't working or flickering
  • You need new power points
  • Appliances need electrical connection
  • Hot water system electrical issues
  • Safety switches keep tripping

You might need both if:

  • Installing a new hot water system
  • Kitchen renovation (plumbing + electrical)
  • Bathroom renovation
  • Laundry appliance installation

What Do Plumbers Do?

Plumbers work with water, gas, and drainage systems.

Common Plumbing Work:

Water Supply:

  • Installing and repairing taps

  • Fixing leaking pipes

  • Water pressure problems

  • Water main connections

  • Rainwater tank plumbing


Drainage:
  • Blocked drains and sewers

  • Stormwater drainage

  • Grease traps

  • Septic systems


Gas:
  • Gas appliance connections (cooktops, ovens, heaters)

  • Gas leak repairs

  • Gas line installation

  • Gas compliance certificates


Hot Water:
  • Hot water system installation (plumbing side)

  • Leak repairs

  • Pressure relief valve replacement

  • Tempering valve installation


Fixtures:
  • Toilets, basins, baths, showers

  • Kitchen sinks and dishwashers

  • Laundry tubs and washing machines


Plumber Licensing in SA:


Licensed plumbers in South Australia hold:
  • Plumbing contractor licence (issued by Consumer and Business Services)

  • Water industry workers card (required for water supply work)

  • Gas fitting licence (if doing gas work)


You can verify a plumber's licence at cbs.sa.gov.au

Common Scenarios: Who Do You Need?

Scenario 1: Hot Water System Not Working

Could be plumbing OR electrical‚Äîhere's how to tell:

Call a plumber if:

  • Water is leaking from the unit

  • No hot water but power is on (could be tempering valve)

  • Gas hot water system issues

  • Pressure relief valve dripping


Call an electrician if:
  • Power supply problem (no power to unit)

  • Electric hot water system not heating (element fault)

  • Circuit breaker keeps tripping when hot water runs

  • Thermostat issues


You'll need both if:
  • Installing a new hot water system (plumber for water/gas, electrician for power)

  • Switching from gas to electric (or vice versa)


---

Scenario 2: Kitchen Appliances

Dishwasher not working:

  • Plumber: For water supply and drainage connections

  • Electrician: For electrical power connection

  • Often need both for installation


Oven or cooktop:
  • Plumber: If gas appliance (gas connection)

  • Electrician: If electric appliance (dedicated circuit required)


Garbage disposal:
  • Plumber: For drainage connection

  • Electrician: For electrical connection

  • Usually need both


---

Scenario 3: Bathroom Renovation

You'll definitely need both:

Plumber for:

  • Moving or installing toilets, basins, baths, showers

  • Drainage and water supply

  • Floor waste

  • Tapware


Electrician for:
  • Exhaust fan installation

  • Lighting (downlights, vanity lights)

  • Heated towel rail electrical

  • Shaver outlets

  • Waterproof electrical fittings


---

Scenario 4: Laundry Appliances

Washing machine:

  • Plumber: Water supply (hot/cold) and drainage

  • Electrician: Only if need new power point or circuit upgrade


Dryer:
  • Plumber: Gas connection (if gas dryer)

  • Electrician: Electrical connection (if electric dryer)


---

Scenario 5: Heating & Cooling

Ducted heating (gas):

  • Plumber: Gas connection

  • Electrician: Power for fan/controller


Ducted air conditioning:
  • Electrician: All electrical work (power, controls)

  • Plumber: Not required (unless hydronic system)


Split system air conditioner:
  • Electrician: All electrical work

  • Plumber: Not required


Hydronic heating:
  • Plumber: All pipework and boiler

  • Electrician: Power supply

Training & Licensing Requirements

Plumbers:

  • 4-year apprenticeship
  • TAFE qualification (Certificate III in Plumbing)
  • Additional gas fitting training (if doing gas work)
  • Minimum 8,000 hours on-the-job training
  • Contractor licence requires additional business training

Electricians:

  • 4-year apprenticeship
  • TAFE qualification (Certificate III in Electrotechnology)
  • Minimum 8,000 hours on-the-job training
  • Must pass electrical licensing exam
  • Contractor licence requires additional business training
Both trades require ongoing professional development and licence renewal.

---

Can One Person Do Both?

No‚Äîin South Australia, plumbing and electrical are separate licensed trades.

You cannot legally do:

  • Electrical work with only a plumbing licence

  • Plumbing work with only an electrical licence


Some contractors employ both plumbers and electricians, which can streamline renovations and coordination.

Anyone claiming to be "licensed for both" is either:

  • Employing separate licensed tradespeople

  • Operating illegally (avoid!)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an electrician connect my dishwasher?

Electrician does the electrical connection, but plumber does the water supply and drainage. You usually need both.

Who installs a hot water system?

Both. Plumber for water/gas, electrician for power. Some companies coordinate both trades.

My lights are flickering‚Äîplumber or electrician?

Electrician‚Äîthat's an electrical fault.

My tap is dripping‚Äîplumber or electrician?

Plumber‚Äîthat's a plumbing issue.

Who installs smoke alarms?

Electrician for hardwired smoke alarms (legally required in SA). Battery-only alarms can be DIY installed.

Can a plumber do electrical in a bathroom?

No‚Äîelectrical work requires an electrical licence, even in bathrooms.

Who connects my new oven?

  • Gas oven: Plumber (gas connection)
  • Electric oven: Electrician (requires dedicated circuit)
