No Power in Half Your House?

One circuit dead while others work? Here's why it happens, how to troubleshoot it yourself, and when to call an electrician.

Quick Diagnosis: Check Your Switchboard

If half your house has no power, one circuit breaker has tripped or failed. Here's what to do:

Step 1: Locate Your Switchboard

Usually in garage, laundry, or external meter box. Open the cover and look at the breakers (switches inside).

Step 2: Look for Switched-Off Breaker

Most breakers have two positions: ON (up) and OFF (down). Look for one that's in the OFF position or somewhere between (tripped).

üí° TIP: Breakers are labeled (or should be) - "Kitchen", "Bedrooms", "Garage", etc. The one that's OFF = the circuit with no power.

Step 3: Try Resetting ONCE

Push the breaker firmly to OFF (down), then back to ON (up). If it clicks and stays on ‚Üí power should restore. If it trips again immediately ‚Üí electrical fault, call electrician.

Why Did My Circuit Trip?

Circuit breakers trip when they detect an electrical fault. Common causes:

  • Overloaded circuit ‚Äì Too many appliances on at once (heaters, kettles, air con)
  • Faulty appliance ‚Äì One appliance with internal short or damaged cord
  • Damaged wiring ‚Äì Cable nicked by nail, rodent damage, insulation breakdown
  • Moisture/water ‚Äì Rain into external outlets, leaking roof, water in roof space
  • Ground fault ‚Äì Electricity leaking to earth (serious‚Äîcall electrician)
  • Loose connection ‚Äì Wire loose in outlet, switch, or switchboard
  • Old breaker failure ‚Äì Breaker itself has failed (common in 20+ year old switchboards)

Troubleshooting: Find the Faulty Appliance

If breaker tripped but resets successfully, try this to identify the problem appliance:

1. Turn Everything Off

Unplug all appliances on the dead circuit. Turn off all switches.

2. Reset Breaker

If it stays on with everything unplugged ‚Üí one of your appliances is faulty.

3. Reconnect One At A Time

Plug in appliances one by one. When the breaker trips ‚Üí that's your faulty appliance. Leave it unplugged and call electrician or replace appliance.

If breaker trips with NOTHING plugged in: The fault is in the wiring itself, not an appliance. This needs professional fault finding. Call an electrician.

When To Call An Electrician

Call immediately if:

  • Breaker won't reset (stays in OFF position)
  • Breaker trips again immediately after reset
  • Breaker trips with nothing plugged in
  • You can't identify which breaker controls the dead circuit
  • You smell burning or see smoke
  • Outlets or switchboard are hot/warm to touch
  • Power went out after storm/water damage
  • Switchboard is old (20+ years) and breakers are rusted/corroded

Adelaide-Specific Issues

Older Homes (Pre-1990s)

Many Adelaide homes have outdated switchboards with old ceramic fuse holders or early-generation breakers. These can fail without warning. If your home is pre-1990s and experiencing partial power loss, likely time for switchboard upgrade.

Storm Damage

Adelaide storms can drive water into external meter boxes, roof spaces, and wall cavities. If power loss started during/after storm, water damage is likely cause. Needs immediate inspection before restoring power.

Air Conditioning Circuit

Older Adelaide homes often have undersized circuits for air con. If circuit trips when air con starts, the circuit might not be rated for the load. Solution: dedicated air con circuit upgrade.
