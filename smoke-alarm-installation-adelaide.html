‚ö†Ô∏è SA Smoke Alarm Legislation - What You Must Have

South Australia has strict smoke alarm requirements. Non-compliance can result in fines and insurance issues.

  • Each storey/level: At least 1 photoelectric alarm on every level of your home
  • Hallways serving bedrooms: 1 alarm in hallways or corridors between bedrooms and living areas
  • Photoelectric only: Ionisation alarms are prohibited - must be photoelectric technology
  • Under 10 years old: Alarms must be replaced every 10 years from manufacture date
  • AS 3786:2014 compliant: All alarms must meet Australian Standard
  • Interconnection (new builds from 2014): Multiple alarms must be interconnected
  • 240V hardwired (post-1995 homes): Properties built after 1 Jan 1995 require mains-powered alarms
  • Rental properties: Landlords legally responsible for installation and maintenance

Why Hardwired Photoelectric Alarms?

Not all smoke alarms are created equal. Here's why we only install hardwired photoelectric alarms:

‚ö° Hardwired = Reliable

Mains-powered with battery backup. No dead batteries at 3am. No forgetting to change batteries. Always operational.

üî¨ Photoelectric = Safer

Detects smouldering fires faster than ionisation alarms. Better for bedrooms (less false alarms from steam/cooking).

üîó Interconnected = Faster

When one alarm detects smoke, ALL alarms sound throughout the house. Critical for large homes or sleeping areas far from fire source.

üìú Compliant = Legal

Meets SA legislation requirements. Certificate of compliance issued. No issues when selling or renting property.
