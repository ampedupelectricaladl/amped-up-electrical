Why Am I Smelling Burning?

Electrical burning smell = something is overheating. Common causes:

1. Overloaded Circuit

Too many appliances on one circuit ‚Üí wiring heats up beyond safe limits ‚Üí insulation starts to burn/melt. Smell is burning plastic (wire insulation).

2. Loose Connections

Wires not properly connected in outlets, switches, or switchboard ‚Üí arcing/sparking ‚Üí heat buildup ‚Üí burning. Very dangerous.

3. Faulty Outlet or Switch

Internal damage, loose terminals, or corrosion ‚Üí high resistance ‚Üí heat ‚Üí burning smell. Often starts with outlet feeling warm to touch.

4. Deteriorating Wiring (Adelaide-Specific)

Adelaide homes built pre-1990s often have cotton-insulated wiring. As insulation deteriorates, wiring can short out and overheat. If your home is old and you smell burning, likely needs rewiring.

5. Damaged Cable in Walls

Rodent damage, nail through wire, or insulation breakdown ‚Üí short circuit ‚Üí heat ‚Üí fire risk. Common in roof spaces and wall cavities.

6. Faulty Appliance

Sometimes the smell is from appliance itself (not wiring). Check if smell is strongest near specific appliance - unplug it immediately.

How Dangerous Is This?

EXTREMELY DANGEROUS.

Electrical burning smell means active overheating. If left unchecked:

  • Fire can start in walls/roof space where you can't see it
  • Fire spreads rapidly through electrical conduits and wall cavities
  • Smoke inhalation risk while you sleep
  • Complete house fire within minutes once materials ignite

Real talk: I've been called to homes where burning smell was ignored for "just a few hours" and found charred wiring inside walls, melted outlet internals, and burn marks on timber framing. You were minutes away from house fire. Don't ignore this.

Adelaide-Specific Risk Factors

Older Homes (Pre-1990s)

Many Adelaide suburbs (Prospect, Norwood, Unley, North Adelaide, etc.) have beautiful older homes‚Äîbut outdated electrical systems. Cotton-insulated wiring breaks down over time. If your home is pre-1990s and you smell burning, high chance it's wiring degradation.

Summer Overload

Adelaide summers = air con running constantly. Older circuits weren't designed for this load. Overheating wiring + heat buildup = perfect fire conditions.

Recent Renovations

If burning smell started after renovation/handyman work, someone might have nicked a cable, overloaded a circuit, or made dodgy connections. Needs immediate inspection.
