Why Does My Power Keep Tripping?

A circuit breaker "trips" (switches off) when it detects an electrical fault. It's a safety feature‚Äîit's doing its job by protecting you.

Common Causes:

  • Overloaded circuit ‚Äì Too many appliances running on one circuit (common: heaters, air con, kettles all at once)
  • Faulty appliance ‚Äì Damaged cord, internal short, or moisture inside appliance
  • Ground fault ‚Äì Electricity leaking to earth (dangerous‚Äîcall immediately)
  • Short circuit ‚Äì Live wire touching neutral or earth
  • Old/deteriorating wiring ‚Äì Insulation breakdown, especially in pre-1990s homes
  • Moisture damage ‚Äì Water in switchboard, outlets, or roof space (common after Adelaide storms)
  • Loose connections ‚Äì Wiring loose in switchboard or outlets

Adelaide-Specific Issues

1. Older Homes (Pre-1990s)

Many Adelaide homes still have cotton-insulated wiring or outdated switchboards. As insulation deteriorates, wiring can short out and cause repeated tripping. If your home is pre-1990s and tripping frequently, you likely need rewiring or switchboard upgrade.

2. Summer Air Conditioning Overload

Adelaide summers = heavy air con use. If your circuit trips when the air con starts, the circuit might be overloaded or undersized. Solution: dedicated air con circuit with correct amperage.

3. Storm Damage

After heavy rain, water can get into switchboards (especially external boards) or roof spaces, causing ground faults. If tripping started after a storm, call an electrician‚Äîwater + electricity = dangerous.

When To Call An Electrician

Call immediately if:

  • Breaker trips more than twice
  • You can't identify which appliance is causing it
  • Breaker trips with nothing plugged in
  • You smell burning or see smoke
  • Outlets or switchboard are hot/warm
  • Tripping started after storm/water damage
  • Your home is pre-1990s (likely wiring issue)
