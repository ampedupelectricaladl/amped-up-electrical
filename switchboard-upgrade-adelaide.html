Why Upgrade Your Switchboard?

  • Old ceramic fuses or rewireable fuses? These don't provide safety switch protection (RCD/RCBO). Upgrade required for safety and compliance.
  • Power keeps tripping? Old switchboards can't handle modern electrical loads. LED lighting, aircon, EV chargers all need capacity.
  • Renovating or selling? Modern switchboards add value and are often required for building approval.
  • Insurance compliance: Some insurers require safety switch protection. Protect your family AND your coverage.
  • Safety switches save lives: RCDs/RCBOs cut power in 30 milliseconds if there's a fault. Old fuseboxes can't do this.

Why RCBOs (Not Basic RCDs)?

Most electricians install basic RCD switchboards with 2-4 shared safety switches protecting multiple circuits. Problem? One faulty appliance trips the whole house. Nightmare trying to find what caused it.

Amped Up installs Hager RCBO switchboards as standard. Every circuit gets its own Hager RCBO (combined safety switch + circuit breaker). Fault on the fridge circuit? Only the fridge trips. Everything else stays on.

The Difference:
  • Basic RCD: 2-4 shared safety switches. One fault = half the house trips. Cheaper but frustrating.
  • RCBO (what we install): Individual protection per circuit. Fault isolation, easier troubleshooting, no nuisance tripping. Modern best practice.

Yes, Hager RCBOs cost $400-600 more than basic RCDs. But you're getting premium German-engineered components, individual circuit protection, easier fault diagnosis, and no more "which appliance is tripping the house?" guessing games. Worth it.

Do I need to upgrade my switchboard?

If you have old ceramic fuses or rewireable fuses, YES. These don't provide RCD protection and are not compliant with current standards. If you're renovating, adding solar/EV charger, or selling, upgrade is often required. We install Hager RCBO switchboards as standard (not basic shared RCDs).

Will my power be off all day?

No. Power is off for 1-2 hours during the switchover. Total job time is 4-6 hours, but most of that is wiring/setup with power on. I schedule around your needs (work from home, kids at home, etc.).
